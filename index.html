About

The Legacy of Nandamuri Taraka Rama Rao (Sr. NTR) Nandamuri Taraka Rama Rao, or **Sr. NTR**, was not just a famous actor, but also a powerful political leader who changed the course of history in Andhra Pradesh. He was loved for his roles in movies and later became a hero for the common people through his leadership in politics.

A Star in Telugu Cinema Born on **May 28, 1923, in Nimmakuru, Andhra Pradesh, NTR started his acting career in 1949. He became a legend in Telugu cinema, known for his powerful portrayals of gods, kings, and warriors. His roles as Lord Krishna and other mythological figures made him a household name. People saw him as a symbol of strength and justice, a reputation that would later help him in his political career.

Entering Politics: A Fight for Telugu Pride By the early 1980s, NTR felt that the people of Andhra Pradesh needed a leader who truly understood their struggles. The ruling Congress party wasn’t meeting the needs of the people, so NTR decided to enter politics. In 1982, he founded the **Telugu Desam Party (TDP)** with the goal of restoring the self-respect of the Telugu people. His slogan, "**Telugu Atma Gauravam**" (Self-respect for Telugus), resonated deeply with the people.

The 1983 Election Victory In 1983, NTR led the TDP in the state elections, and against all odds, his party won in a huge way. NTR became the **Chief Minister of Andhra Pradesh**, marking a new era in state politics. He promised to fight for the poor, bring an end to corruption, and improve the lives of farmers, women, and backward communities.

Key Reforms by NTR

Once in power, NTR made several important changes:

  1. Welfare for Farmers**: NTR launched programs to help farmers with subsidies and loan waivers.
  2. Food for the Poor**: He introduced a subsidized rice scheme to help feed the poor.
  3. Support for Women**: NTR worked on laws to protect women and improve their rights.
  4. Corruption-Free Government**: He focused on reducing corruption in the government and making it more transparent.

The 1984 Setback and Comeback

However, politics is full of surprises. In 1984, NTR was unexpectedly removed from office by a group of people in his own party. Despite this setback, NTR made a remarkable comeback in 1985, leading the TDP to a victory in the next elections. This comeback proved his deep connection with the people of Andhra Pradesh.

His Lasting Impact

Even after his passing in 1996, NTR’s legacy continues. His son, **N. Chandrababu Naidu**, followed in his footsteps and became the Chief Minister of Andhra Pradesh, continuing his father’s work. NTR is remembered for his courage, vision, and dedication to the people.

He remains an iconic figure in both cinema and politics, a man who fought for the people’s rights and gave them a voice. NTR's name will always be remembered as a symbol of pride and strength for the Telugu people.

Sr. NTR, the iconic actor, director, and former Chief Minister of Andhra Pradesh, achieved monumental success in both his film career and political life. Below are some of his remarkable achievements:

NTR acted in over 300 Telugu films during his illustrious career, earning the title "Nata Samrat" (Emperor of Acting).
Revolutionized Telugu Cinema:

As a filmmaker, NTR introduced new trends in the industry, from storytelling to technological advancements.
Received several awards:
He was honored with the Padma Shri and the Padma Bhushan awards for his contribution to Indian cinema and culture.

Political Achievements:

NTR founded the TDP in 1982, giving voice to the Telugu-speaking people and bringing a new wave of regional politics.
Chief Minister of Andhra Pradesh:
He served as the Chief Minister of Andhra Pradesh three times and played a vital role in shaping the state's modern political landscape.
Introduced welfare schemes:
NTR implemented several groundbreaking welfare programs such as the "Rs. 2-a-kilo rice" scheme, which helped the underprivileged.
Enhanced regional pride:
His leadership fostered a sense of pride in Telugu culture, language, and traditions, significantly influencing regional politics in India.
Introduced land reforms:
He introduced various land reforms, ensuring the distribution of land to the landless poor, making him a champion for rural development.
Legacy:
Sr. NTR's legacy continues to inspire generations of actors, politicians, and leaders. His contributions to both cinema and politics remain unparalleled, cementing his place as one of the most influential figures in Indian history.

His contribution to the Indian film industry is immense, and his legacy in both cinema and politics continues to inspire many. Here’s an overview of the major movies acted by Senior NTR, highlighting his journey and impact.

1. NTR: The Early Years (1949-1960s)
NTR's journey into acting began in the late 1940s, but it was in the 1950s that he rose to prominence. Known for his dynamic performances and powerful screen presence, NTR became an icon with his portrayal of mythological and historical characters. He played roles that ranged from gods to kings, and his performances often carried deep moral messages. "Mana Desam" (1949): NTR’s film debut was in this patriotic drama. It set the stage for what would become a remarkable career. "Palletoori Pilla" (1950): A comedy that showcased NTR's versatility early on.
2. The Mythological Era (1960s-1970s)
The 1960s and 1970s saw NTR take on mythological roles, many of which became career-defining performances. He brought life to several Hindu mythological figures, turning them into household names. "Sri Krishna Pandaviyam" (1966): NTR's portrayal of Lord Krishna was lauded for its depth, and the film became one of his most popular mythological films. "Nartanasala" (1963): A film based on the Mahabharata, NTR’s portrayal of Arjuna was widely celebrated. The 1960s marked a period when NTR became synonymous with legendary roles, especially portraying gods like Rama, Krishna, and other mythological figures. His divine depictions made him a household name and an icon in Telugu cinema.
3. The Social Dramas and Hero Roles (1970s-1980s)
In the late 70s and 80s, NTR transitioned into more realistic roles in social dramas and heroic films, showcasing his versatility. He began portraying strong, justice-driven characters who fought against corruption, social injustice, and oppression. "Bobbili Puli" (1982): NTR played the role of a warrior who seeks revenge for the wrongs done to his family. This was one of the films that highlighted his action-oriented roles. "Sitarama Kalyanam" (1983): A film showcasing NTR's portrayal of Lord Rama, once again solidifying his status as a legendary actor in mythological roles. His characterizations were always larger-than-life, and his speeches and dialogues were delivered with gravitas, often leaving a lasting impact on the audience.
4. Political Roles in Cinema (1980s-1990s)
By the 1980s, NTR had cemented his reputation as a hero both on and off-screen. After founding the Telugu Desam Party (TDP) in 1982, he continued to dominate both the political and cinematic worlds. He merged his political persona with his film roles, often portraying characters that stood up for the common man. "Yamagola" (1977): NTR's role as a fearless man who challenges Yama (the god of death) was a defining moment in his career. This film was a mix of action, comedy, and sentiment. "Sivayya" (1988): In this action-packed drama, NTR played a character who fights for justice and against injustice. During this period, NTR’s films were marked by themes of morality, bravery, and empowerment, aligning with his political ideology.
5. Later Years and Legacy (1990s-1997)
Even in his later years, NTR continued to take on roles that kept him relevant in the industry. His films in the 90s may not have had the same massive impact as his earlier works, but they still reflected the maturity and depth he had gained throughout his career.
"NTR's Kshana Kshana" (1991):
A movie that reflected his continuing appeal among the masses, even in the 90s. "Varasudu" (1993): A family drama where NTR's character emphasized values such as love, family, and duty. Senior NTR passed away in 1997, but his cinematic legacy lives on. His influence can still be seen in the way Telugu cinema portrays heroic characters.

NTR’s Impact on Telugu Cinema and Legacy Senior NTR's filmography includes over 300 films in a career spanning five decades. His contributions to Telugu cinema were immeasurable. NTR was not just an actor; he was a phenomenon that redefined the Telugu film industry. His portrayal of mythological characters and his larger-than-life screen presence made him an icon of Indian cinema.

In addition to his acting skills, NTR was also a master of dialogue delivery, and his speeches in films were often delivered with an intensity that captivated the audience. His dedication to his roles and his ability to connect with the masses made him a beloved figure in South Indian cinema.

NTR's legacy continues through his family, particularly his son, Nandamuri Balakrishna, who followed in his footsteps to become a celebrated actor in Telugu cinema.
