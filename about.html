The Legacy of Nandamuri Taraka Rama Rao (Sr. NTR) Nandamuri Taraka Rama Rao, or **Sr. NTR**, was not just a famous actor, but also a powerful political leader who changed the course of history in Andhra Pradesh. He was loved for his roles in movies and later became a hero for the common people through his leadership in politics.

A Star in Telugu Cinema Born on **May 28, 1923, in Nimmakuru, Andhra Pradesh, NTR started his acting career in 1949. He became a legend in Telugu cinema, known for his powerful portrayals of gods, kings, and warriors. His roles as Lord Krishna and other mythological figures made him a household name. People saw him as a symbol of strength and justice, a reputation that would later help him in his political career.

Entering Politics: A Fight for Telugu Pride By the early 1980s, NTR felt that the people of Andhra Pradesh needed a leader who truly understood their struggles. The ruling Congress party wasn’t meeting the needs of the people, so NTR decided to enter politics. In 1982, he founded the **Telugu Desam Party (TDP)** with the goal of restoring the self-respect of the Telugu people. His slogan, "**Telugu Atma Gauravam**" (Self-respect for Telugus), resonated deeply with the people.

The 1983 Election Victory In 1983, NTR led the TDP in the state elections, and against all odds, his party won in a huge way. NTR became the **Chief Minister of Andhra Pradesh**, marking a new era in state politics. He promised to fight for the poor, bring an end to corruption, and improve the lives of farmers, women, and backward communities.

Key Reforms by NTR

Once in power, NTR made several important changes:

  1. Welfare for Farmers**: NTR launched programs to help farmers with subsidies and loan waivers.
  2. Food for the Poor**: He introduced a subsidized rice scheme to help feed the poor.
  3. Support for Women**: NTR worked on laws to protect women and improve their rights.
  4. Corruption-Free Government**: He focused on reducing corruption in the government and making it more transparent.

The 1984 Setback and Comeback

However, politics is full of surprises. In 1984, NTR was unexpectedly removed from office by a group of people in his own party. Despite this setback, NTR made a remarkable comeback in 1985, leading the TDP to a victory in the next elections. This comeback proved his deep connection with the people of Andhra Pradesh.

His Lasting Impact

Even after his passing in 1996, NTR’s legacy continues. His son, **N. Chandrababu Naidu**, followed in his footsteps and became the Chief Minister of Andhra Pradesh, continuing his father’s work. NTR is remembered for his courage, vision, and dedication to the people.

He remains an iconic figure in both cinema and politics, a man who fought for the people’s rights and gave them a voice. NTR's name will always be remembered as a symbol of pride and strength for the Telugu people.
