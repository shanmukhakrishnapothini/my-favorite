Nandamuri Taraka Rama Rao (NTR), fondly known as Senior NTR, was one of the most iconic figures in Telugu cinema. He was not just an actor but also a legendary politician. His contribution to the Indian film industry is immense, and his legacy in both cinema and politics continues to inspire many. Here’s an overview of the major movies acted by Senior NTR, highlighting his journey and impact.

1. NTR: The Early Years (1949-1960s)
NTR's journey into acting began in the late 1940s, but it was in the 1950s that he rose to prominence. Known for his dynamic performances and powerful screen presence, NTR became an icon with his portrayal of mythological and historical characters. He played roles that ranged from gods to kings, and his performances often carried deep moral messages. "Mana Desam" (1949): NTR’s film debut was in this patriotic drama. It set the stage for what would become a remarkable career. "Palletoori Pilla" (1950): A comedy that showcased NTR's versatility early on.
2. The Mythological Era (1960s-1970s)
The 1960s and 1970s saw NTR take on mythological roles, many of which became career-defining performances. He brought life to several Hindu mythological figures, turning them into household names. "Sri Krishna Pandaviyam" (1966): NTR's portrayal of Lord Krishna was lauded for its depth, and the film became one of his most popular mythological films. "Nartanasala" (1963): A film based on the Mahabharata, NTR’s portrayal of Arjuna was widely celebrated. The 1960s marked a period when NTR became synonymous with legendary roles, especially portraying gods like Rama, Krishna, and other mythological figures. His divine depictions made him a household name and an icon in Telugu cinema.
3. The Social Dramas and Hero Roles (1970s-1980s)
In the late 70s and 80s, NTR transitioned into more realistic roles in social dramas and heroic films, showcasing his versatility. He began portraying strong, justice-driven characters who fought against corruption, social injustice, and oppression. "Bobbili Puli" (1982): NTR played the role of a warrior who seeks revenge for the wrongs done to his family. This was one of the films that highlighted his action-oriented roles. "Sitarama Kalyanam" (1983): A film showcasing NTR's portrayal of Lord Rama, once again solidifying his status as a legendary actor in mythological roles. His characterizations were always larger-than-life, and his speeches and dialogues were delivered with gravitas, often leaving a lasting impact on the audience.
4. Political Roles in Cinema (1980s-1990s)
By the 1980s, NTR had cemented his reputation as a hero both on and off-screen. After founding the Telugu Desam Party (TDP) in 1982, he continued to dominate both the political and cinematic worlds. He merged his political persona with his film roles, often portraying characters that stood up for the common man. "Yamagola" (1977): NTR's role as a fearless man who challenges Yama (the god of death) was a defining moment in his career. This film was a mix of action, comedy, and sentiment. "Sivayya" (1988): In this action-packed drama, NTR played a character who fights for justice and against injustice. During this period, NTR’s films were marked by themes of morality, bravery, and empowerment, aligning with his political ideology.
5. Later Years and Legacy (1990s-1997)
Even in his later years, NTR continued to take on roles that kept him relevant in the industry. His films in the 90s may not have had the same massive impact as his earlier works, but they still reflected the maturity and depth he had gained throughout his career.
"NTR's Kshana Kshana" (1991):
A movie that reflected his continuing appeal among the masses, even in the 90s. "Varasudu" (1993): A family drama where NTR's character emphasized values such as love, family, and duty. Senior NTR passed away in 1997, but his cinematic legacy lives on. His influence can still be seen in the way Telugu cinema portrays heroic characters.

NTR’s Impact on Telugu Cinema and Legacy Senior NTR's filmography includes over 300 films in a career spanning five decades. His contributions to Telugu cinema were immeasurable. NTR was not just an actor; he was a phenomenon that redefined the Telugu film industry. His portrayal of mythological characters and his larger-than-life screen presence made him an icon of Indian cinema.

In addition to his acting skills, NTR was also a master of dialogue delivery, and his speeches in films were often delivered with an intensity that captivated the audience. His dedication to his roles and his ability to connect with the masses made him a beloved figure in South Indian cinema.

NTR's legacy continues through his family, particularly his son, Nandamuri Balakrishna, who followed in his footsteps to become a celebrated actor in Telugu cinema.
